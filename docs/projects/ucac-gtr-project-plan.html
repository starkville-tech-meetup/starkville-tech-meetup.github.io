GTR Tech Connect × UCAC Starkville

The Community Bridge
Project Plan

A tech-forward strategy to connect Starkville-area farmers with community members, institutions, and Mississippi State University — sustainably.

Initiated: April 2026
Lead Groups: GTR Tech Connect + UCAC
Scope: Starkville, MS + Oktibbeha County
01 — Context

The Problem Worth Solving

Starkville's local farmers have something rare: real food, real land, and real community investment. What they're missing is the connective tissue — a reliable, low-friction way to tell the community "here's what we have, here's when we have it, and here's how to get it."

The gap isn't agricultural. It's informational. And it's fixable with the right tools built the right way.

Mississippi State University — one of the country's leading ag research institutions — is sitting right next door with students, faculty, extension programs, and departments that want exactly these community connections. They just don't have a clear front door into it either.

GTR Tech Connect's role: build that front door. Maintain it. Hand the keys to the people it was built for.

02 — Solution Overview

Platform: Starkville Harvest Hub

A lightweight, multi-channel platform — not one monolithic app — that meets farmers and community members where they already are. The core of the platform is a community marketplace + resource directory with three primary channels:

🌐
Web Portal
Simple public-facing site. Farmer listings, available inventory, seasonal schedules, MSU connection requests. Mobile-first, no login required to browse.
📱
SMS / WhatsApp Alerts
Farmers text availability updates. Community members subscribe for "what's available this week" notifications. Zero smartphone skill required to participate as a farmer.
🏫
MSU Partner Portal
Dedicated view for MSU extension programs, agriculture departments, student orgs, and dining services to request, partner, and source from local farms.
📋
Admin Dashboard
For UCAC coordinators and GTR volunteers — manage listings, review requests, track inventory donations, and monitor platform health without needing to code.
03 — Architecture

Recommended Tech Stack

Chosen for low cost, high maintainability, and realistic volunteer capacity. Nothing that requires a full-time dev to keep alive.

Frontend / Web

Next.js (React) Tailwind CSS Vercel (hosting)

Backend / Database

Supabase (Postgres + Auth) Node.js API layer Prisma ORM

Communications

Twilio (SMS) WhatsApp Business API Mailchimp (newsletter)

Data & Admin

Airtable (non-tech admin view) Power BI (optional reporting) GitHub (version control) Azure DevOps (optional CI/CD)

💡 Airtable as the farmer-facing data entry layer keeps things simple for UCAC coordinators to manage without touching code. It syncs to Supabase via automation or manual export cadence.

05 — People

Key Stakeholders & Roles

UCAC Farmers
Core Beneficiaries + Owners
Provide inventory data, validate platform usefulness, eventually take ownership of their own listings. Their buy-in is the whole game.
GTR Tech Connect
Builders + Maintainers
Design, build, and maintain the platform. Provide volunteer dev hours. Rotate members to avoid single points of failure.
MSU Extension Service
Institutional Partner
Natural ally — they exist to bridge ag research and community. Can amplify reach, provide expertise, and connect student volunteers.
MSU Ag & Food Depts
Demand Side Partners
Food Science, Dining Services, student food orgs — these become reliable buyers and project collaborators with proper onboarding.
UCAC Coordinator
Community Liaison
Bridge between tech team and farmers. Attends GTR syncs. Escalates farmer issues. Ensures platform reflects real community needs, not assumed ones.
Starkville Community
End Users + Subscribers
Families, churches, restaurants, neighbors who want access to local food. They're the demand that motivates farmers to keep the platform active.

07 — Risk Management

Know the Risks, Plan the Mitigations

Risk Level Mitigation
Farmers don't adopt the platform / trust issues HIGH Build WITH farmers in Phase 1 before building FOR them. Co-name the platform. Make SMS the primary farmer interface — no app, no login.
GTR volunteer burnout / key person dependency HIGH Write runbooks from day one. Rotate responsibilities. Design for handoff, not heroics. Choose boring, maintainable tech.
MSU partnership stalls in bureaucracy MED Start with Extension Service — they're structurally built for this. Student orgs can move faster than departments. Have a live platform before first meeting.
Platform costs become unsustainable MED Vercel free tier + Supabase free tier covers MVP. Target under $30/month running cost. Pursue USDA/MDA grants in Phase 4 to cover long-term ops.
Low community subscriber engagement LOW Partner with existing channels: church bulletins, Starkville FB community groups, MSU student orgs. People want local food — they just need to know it exists.
